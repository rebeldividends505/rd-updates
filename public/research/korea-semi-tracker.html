How To Read This Data
The leading indicator logic: Samsung and SK Hynix represent ~70% of global DRAM/HBM production. When their export data moves, it reflects actual hyperscaler purchase orders — weeks before earnings guidance and months before analyst revisions. The SOX index tends to peak 1-3 months after Korean semi export growth peaks.

What to watch: The rate of change matters more than the absolute level. +182% looks great until it becomes +140%, then +90%, then +40%. Each step down in YoY growth rate is a step down in the cycle. The blow-off top in exports (March/April 2026) historically precedes the SOX top by 1-3 months — and the SOX already started correcting before the macro data peaked.

The SOXL-specific problem: At 85% annualized volatility and 3x daily leverage, SOXL loses ~20% per year to volatility decay even if SOX goes sideways. You need a genuinely rising SOX just to break even. Late-cycle entry into SOXL is a negative-expected-value trade on a long enough horizon.
