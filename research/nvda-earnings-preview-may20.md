## 4. What to Watch in the Call — Bullish vs Bearish Phrases

### BULLISH phrases — buy the dip on these
1. **"Demand continues to significantly exceed supply"** — repeat of the standard line; if it disappears, that's the tell.
2. **"Blackwell Ultra is ramping ahead of plan"** or **"yields are progressing well"** — confirms Q2/Q3 step-function.
3. **"Sovereign AI represents a multi-tens-of-billions opportunity over the next several quarters"** — specific dollar-sizing of UAE/KSA/India deals.
4. **"H20 visibility into China has improved"** — un-locks $5–8B of incremental revenue currently in limbo.
5. **"We are sold out for the year"** — Jensen has said this before; if he says it on this call about Rubin (next-gen), that's the bull dream.
6. **"HBM supply remains the gating factor"** — bullish for MU/SK Hynix, neutral-to-slightly-bearish for NVDA near-term but very bullish for the duration of the cycle.
7. **"Inference is now larger than training"** — confirms the secular shift; bullish for B300/Rubin demand durability.

### BEARISH phrases — trim on these
1. **"Lumpy"** or **"non-linear"** — any version of "quarter-to-quarter variability" applied to revenue or sovereign deals.
2. **"Working through inventory"** at hyperscalers — first sign of capex digestion.
3. **"Pacing our shipments to match supply chain readiness"** — code for "we have a yield problem".
4. **"H20 China remains uncertain"** or any non-affirmative China commentary.
5. **"Gross margin will trend toward the mid-70s"** without an explicit timeline — soft on near-term margin.
6. **"We expect a transition quarter"** — death sentence for the multiple.
7. Any explicit mention of a named hyperscaler **reducing**, **rephasing**, or **optimizing** orders.

### Things to specifically count
- **Number of times "Sovereign" is said** (>10 = bullish, <5 = soft)
- **Number of named hyperscalers in the prepared remarks** (the more, the better — silence on one is a tell)
- **Whether Jensen quantifies Rubin in dollars** (he hasn't yet — if he does, that's a major bull signal for 2027 estimates)
- **CFO Colette Kress's tone on networking revenue** (Spectrum-X / Quantum InfiniBand) — networking attach is the highest-margin lever

## 5. Recommendation — Buy Before, or Wait?

### **WAIT-AND-SEE** — but with nuance.

**The case for waiting:**
1. **The chart has done the work for the bulls.** NVDA is +24.9% in 30 sessions into the print at a 52-week high. Buying here is buying the same thing the algos have been buying for a month.
2. **Consensus is unanimous and aggressive.** 93% Buy-or-better and FY27 EPS at $8.28 already prices a smooth Blackwell ramp. The bar is high.
3. **Asymmetry is unfavorable.** Beat-and-raise is +3% net by Friday; soft guide is -8%; miss is -15%. Risk/reward is 1:3 against you at $219 vs. $200.
4. **Implied vol is rich.** ATM straddles for the May 22 expiry are pricing **~7.5% move**. Selling premium (covered calls, put credit spreads on a pullback) is better than long stock here.
5. **The "easy YoY comp" trap.** The +70% YoY headline is going to be widely cheered, but sophisticated money will be looking at **sequential growth** (Q4 $68B → Q1 ~$76B = +12% q/q). That's good — but it's not the kind of number that justifies a +5% post-print pop on top of a 25% run-up.

**Conditions under which we'd buy before:**
- A 3–5% pullback into $208–212 before the close on 5/20 (rebuilds the asymmetry)
- A clearly bearish setup in semis on print day (SOX down 2%+) that we think is wrong
- A specific China H20 catalyst (export license expansion) leaked in the last 48 hours

**Conditions under which we'd buy after — even on a beat:**
- Beat-and-raise that gets faded to $208 or lower on Thursday (Scenario A "fade" outcome) — this is the highest-probability good entry
- In-line print sold off to $195–200 (Scenario B) — adds the next-12-month trade with the 50-dma as support
- A clean Q2 guide above $85B regardless of immediate reaction

**Conditions under which we'd stay away entirely:**
- Any "lumpy", "non-linear", "transition quarter", or named-hyperscaler softness in the prepared remarks
- Gross margin print below 73% with no specific Blackwell-yield bridge to the mid-70s by Q3
- Jensen sounding defensive on the Q&A (style tell — he is usually electric on calls)

### The bigger frame
The NVDA story is intact for the next 18–24 months. Blackwell Ultra → Rubin → Vera Rubin is a multi-year backlog. The trade question for May 20 isn't *"is NVDA a good company?"* — it's *"is +24.9% in 30 sessions into a fully-buy-rated print the right time to add?"* The answer is almost always **no**. The right time to add to NVDA is when the consensus that's already buy-rated is one bad print away from going neutral — and we are nowhere near that.

**Bottom line:** Trim into print, hedge if exposed, and prepare bids in the $200–212 zone for Thursday/Friday. The setup favors patience, not anticipation.
