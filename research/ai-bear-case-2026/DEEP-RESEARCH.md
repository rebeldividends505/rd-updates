## Topic 4: Physical AI Buildout Bottlenecks

### Key findings

**Power grid capacity is the binding constraint:**
- **12 GW of new data center power announced for 2026 — only 5 GW under construction. ~7 GW stalled** due to grid/power issues (Sightline Climate via Tech Investments).
- 240+ GW announced through 2030 by hyperscalers/cloud providers — equal to ~20% of current US electricity demand.
- **Gartner: 40% of AI data centers will be power-constrained by 2027.**
- Single AI task consumes up to 1,000x more electricity than a traditional search.
- Site sizes have ballooned to **1-4 GW per new site** (vs. legacy 50-100 MW).

**Transformer / grid-tie crisis:**
- **5-year backlog on grid transformers** — directly "killing half of America's 2026 AI data centers" (Tech Investments).
- High-voltage transformers, switchgear, and grid-tie batteries all in supply-chain crisis.
- **Interconnect queues: 5-7 years.** A data center announced in 2026 can't grid-connect before 2031.
- **Transformer lead times up to 72 months** per iRecruit data center cost reporting.

**Regional bottlenecks:**
- **Northern Virginia:** July 2024 voltage fluctuation disconnected **60 data centers**, created 1,500 MW surplus, nearly cascade-failed the grid. "Data Center Alley" is now grid-constrained.
- **Texas (ERCOT):** Rapid AI buildout straining grid; permitting + political delays for 1-4 GW sites.
- **Phoenix (Arizona):** "Massive power demand strains regional grid" — example of operational limits already hit. 100+ kW/rack density.

**GPU lead times (May 2026):**
- **H100 PCIe: 2-4 weeks via OEMs** (down from prior 6-9 months — supply has eased for this older SKU).
- **H100 SXM5: 36-52 weeks from resellers.**

- B200 allocated through H2 2027 per Spheron.

- New Blackwell deployments stretching to June-July 2026.

- **TSMC CoWoS capacity is fully allocated.** Root cause of GPU shortages alongside SK Hynix HBM limits.
- H100 manufacturing cost ~$3,300, sells for ~$28K (88% gross margin); B200 cost ~$6,400.
