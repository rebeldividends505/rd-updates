# Macro Data — AI Rotation Thesis, May 2026

## 1. Valuation: We are at the second-highest reading in 155 years

- **Shiller CAPE (May 1, 2026): 40.11** (other sources show 42.05 intra-month). The CAPE has now exceeded 40 for the first time since the dot-com bubble. The only prior reading higher than today was **November 1999 at 44.19**.
- **Median historical CAPE: 16.05.** We are 2.5x the historical median.
- **S&P 500 forward P/E: 23x.** Above any non-bubble period in recorded history.
- Implication: every historical CAPE reading above 35 has been followed by sub-par 7-10 year real returns. November 1999's reading was followed by a 49% S&P 500 drawdown and a 78% NASDAQ drawdown.

## 2. Concentration risk: Five stocks ARE the market

- The top 5 S&P 500 names = **30%+ of the index** — highest concentration in 50 years.
- The Magnificent 7 = 30%+ of total S&P 500 value.
- NVDA, MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN, AVGO, META = ~30% of the S&P 500 by themselves.
- AI infrastructure & related = roughly one-third of all 2025 S&P 500 gains.
- For comparison: March 2000's tech-sector peak weighting was 33% of the S&P 500. We are at par or above with the dot-com peak.
- **BofA (Savita Subramanian) 2026 year-end S&P target: 7,100** vs. current ~6,864 — only 4% upside. Most bearish major Wall Street view, explicitly citing AI valuations.

## 3. Capex math is breaking — the cash flow story

Four hyperscalers' 2026 capex guides:

| Company    | 2026 Capex | FCF Impact (Street estimate)            |
|------------|------------|------------------------------------------|
| Amazon     | $200B      | Negative $17B (MS) to negative $28B (BofA) FCF for the year |
| Microsoft  | $190B      | FCF down 28% (Barclays)                 |
| Alphabet   | $185B      | FCF "weakest in a decade"; trends toward 0 in 2027 (asymco) |
| Meta       | $125–145B  | FCF down ~90% (Barclays)                |
| **Combined** | **~$700–805B** | Combined hyperscaler FCF could drop to **$4B in Q3** vs. $45B/qtr post-COVID average |

- Bank of America forecasts hyperscaler debt issuance hits **$175B in 2026** vs. $28B/yr average for the prior five years. Six-fold increase.
- Microsoft alone attributes $25B of its 2026 capex bump to memory/chip cost inflation — meaning even the hyperscalers are paying the suppliers' bubble premium.
- This is the breaking-point indicator: when the customers funding the AI buildout have to issue 6x more debt to do it, the buildout is by definition past its peak ROIC moment.

## 4. The dot-com 2000 precedent — what came next

- NASDAQ peaked **March 10, 2000 at 5,048.62.**
- By October 2002: NASDAQ -78%, S&P 500 -49%.
- $5 trillion in market value wiped out.
- Tech sector weighting in S&P 500 collapsed from 33% (June 2000) to under 15% by 2003.
- Venture-capital funding contracted from $112.3B peak (2000) to a multi-year freeze.
- What rallied: gold (+12% in 2000 alone after the peak), bonds, energy, financials — basically anything that was NOT tech.
- Crypto did not exist yet, but the rotation precedent (capital leaving the most-crowded trade) is the template.

## 5. Bitcoin / M2 setup — divergence is the trigger

- Bitcoin price (May 10, 2026): **$80,606.**
- Bitcoin ATH: **$126,198.07 (Oct 6, 2025).** Currently **-36% from ATH.**
- Global M2 YoY: **+10%.** BTC YoY: negative. Wide divergence.
- Historical pattern: BTC has tracked global M2 with a ~10-week lag. The current decoupling is the widest since 2022 bottom and is either:
  (a) a top signal for liquidity-driven assets (bearish case), OR
  (b) a setup where BTC catches back up to liquidity (bullish case — base case here).
- Either way, BTC has **already had its 36% correction** while AI stocks have not.
- This asymmetry is the rotation thesis in one sentence: take profits where the correction has NOT happened, redeploy into where it has.

## 6. Yen carry trade — the systemic tail risk

- BOJ benchmark rate: **0.75%** (highest in 30 years), with more hikes signaled.
- Outstanding yen carry positions estimated at **$261B–$500B** (Morgan Stanley), with yen forwards held by trading companies/hedge funds at **¥35 trillion** (BCA Research, Oct 2025).
- Assets most exposed to unwind: leveraged equities, EM bonds, crypto.
- August 2024 mini-unwind triggered the year's worst single-day risk-off across SPX, NASDAQ, BTC. A 2026 unwind could be 3x larger per LiveMediaNews analysis.
- This is the macro accelerant: if yen carry unwinds, AI stocks lead the way down because they are the most-crowded, most-leveraged trade in the world.
