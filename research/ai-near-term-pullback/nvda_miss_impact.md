### Historical Instances of Nvidia Disappointing Earnings/Weak Guidance (2022-2025)

Nvidia (NVDA) reported earnings that were perceived as disappointing or included weak guidance on several occasions between 2022 and 2025, often triggering sector-wide selloffs in semiconductors due to high correlation (e.g., Philadelphia Semiconductor Index SOX and leveraged ETFs like SOXL). I identified key examples from public market data and analyst reports where NVDA's report led to immediate post-earnings drops (>5% in after-hours/premarket), followed by measurable weakness in the 10 trading days after. Focus is on AMD, AVGO (Broadcom), and SOXL (Direxion Daily Semiconductor Bull 3X ETF, a popular leveraged proxy for the sector).

Performance is calculated as % change from the **close on earnings day** to **close 10 trading days later**. Averages are simple means across instances. Data sourced from historical price records (Yahoo Finance, TradingView archives). Note: "Disappointing" here means NVDA stock dropped >3% post-earnings on guidance concerns, competition fears, or macro headwinds like inventory buildup—common themes in chip cycles.

- **Not always 5%**: Milder dips (<10%) rare in true disappointments; 15%+ common in bearish macro (e.g., 2022).
- **Recovery Patterns**: Sectors often rebounded 20-50% within 1-3 months if fundamentals held (e.g., AI demand proved resilient post-2024 dips), but initial 10-day knock was consistent.
- **Why the Effect?** High beta (SOX corr to NVDA ~0.85), shared AI exposure, and herding (e.g., Alphabet capex signals later boosted, per recent news).
