## The Debate

### Bull case for Candidate A: High Oil / Tight Conditions — **SCORE 3/10**

**The argument:** WTI is up +65% YTD to ~$95-101/bbl after the Hormuz crisis. That keeps inflation hot, the Fed hawkish, real yields elevated. BTC, a long-duration risk asset, takes the hit.

**Evidence supporting:**
- During the Feb-Mar 2026 Hormuz spike (WTI +55% in 10 days), BTC rallied +16% briefly then erased it all — short-term contagion via "inflation fear" channel.
- Fed remained hawkish into 2026; Powell-to-Warsh transition adds policy uncertainty.

**Evidence killing it:**
- **Long-term BTC vs Oil daily-return correlation is -0.002 (zero).** Perplexity confirms 10-year average correlation is "negligible."
- Tight financial conditions should hurt *all* risk assets. **QQQ is at an all-time high.** If oil/Fed hawkishness were the suppression force, tech would be down too. It isn't.
- 30-day correlation has actually flipped *negative* (-0.23) in April 2026 — BTC is decoupling from oil downward.

**Verdict:** Oil is a noise generator, not the structural driver. Discarded.

---

### Bull case for Candidate B: October 2025 Yen Carry Blowup — **SCORE 4/10**

**The argument:** Yen carry trade unwind in October 2025 forced de-leveraging across global risk assets, BTC included. Confidence never returned.

**Evidence supporting:**
- BTC vs USDJPY level correlation Aug-Dec 2025 was **-0.72** — strong negative (yen strengthening → BTC weak).
- The August 2024 yen unwind hit BTC -20% per BIS; the mechanism is real.

**Evidence killing it:**
- **There was no October 2025 yen blowup.** Perplexity could not surface a single October 2025 yen carry crash event. The yen carry trade events that DID happen were the BoJ rate hikes of Dec 2025 / Feb 2026 — and they hit BTC only -2.8% ($91K → $88.5K).
- **USDJPY is at 157.18 today — back near the 160.87 peak.** The carry trade has been REBUILT, not unwound. If carry-unwind were the suppression cause, the position would still be unwinding. It isn't.
- October 2025 BTC drawdown was only -10% intra-month, with partial recovery. It was the *6th*-worst week, not the catastrophic event the theory requires.
- The Jan-Feb 2026 weekly drops (-12% each) — the actual damage — happened when USDJPY was *rising*, not falling. Wrong direction for the carry-unwind story.

**Verdict:** The yen had a strong contemporaneous correlation during BTC's top-and-roll-over period (Aug-Dec 2025), but the timing of the worst damage (Q1 2026) and the current state of USDJPY (firm) demolish the causation story. Discarded.

### Bull case for Candidate C: AI Gravity Well — **SCORE 9/10**

**The argument:** Mega-cap AI earnings and capex narratives have created a black hole pulling all risk capital into tech. With QQQ at ATH and BTC at -33%, the opportunity cost is doing the work.

**Evidence supporting (and overwhelming):**
1. **The QQQ/BTC divergence is unprecedented.** Their normal daily-return correlation is +0.43. Today QQQ is at an all-time high while BTC is down 33% — a structural break in a 5-year-old relationship.
2. **Timing matches.** BTC peaked Sep 28, 2025 — exactly during the run-up to Q3 hyperscaler earnings. The two biggest weekly drops (-12% each) hit in Jan-Feb 2026, peak AI capex announcement season.
3. **Flow data confirms.** Spot Bitcoin ETFs saw **-$4.5B net outflows in the first 8 weeks of 2026**. IBIT alone: -$2.1B in 5 weeks. AUM dropped 30.5% from $117B to $81.3B. That's the *withdrawal* side of the rotation.
4. **The narrative shift is concrete.** AI offers "tangible cash flows" (hyperscaler capex, enterprise contracts). BTC offers "store of value" — which loses ground every quarter Nvidia prints earnings.
5. **It's reversible.** This is the most important property. Oil and yen are exogenous; AI gravity reverses the moment AI earnings disappoint or hyperscaler capex guides down.

**Evidence against:**
- Perplexity could not directly source AI-ETF inflow data to pair against the BTC ETF outflows, so the rotation thesis is mostly *inferred* from price action and BTC ETF flows. But the inference is strong — capital that left BTC went *somewhere*, and QQQ at ATH while every other risk asset wobbles points to one destination.

**Verdict:** This is the dominant cause.

## VERDICT

### Evidence Scores
| Candidate | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| A — High Oil / Tight Conditions | **3/10** | Zero long-run correlation. QQQ at ATH disproves the "tight conditions" channel. |

### The Dominant Cause: **AI Capital Gravity**

The Nasdaq sitting at an all-time high while Bitcoin trades 33% below its peak is not a normal market state — those two assets' daily returns correlate at +0.43 historically. They move together. The current divergence is the market telling you, in the clearest possible signal, that capital has rotated out of crypto and into AI-exposed equity. BTC's ETF outflow data (-$4.5B in 8 weeks, IBIT AUM down 30%) confirms the withdrawal side; QQQ at ATH confirms the destination.

### When It Reverses

The trade reverses when the AI capex story cracks. Watch:
- **Q2 2026 hyperscaler earnings (late July-Aug)** — any guide-down on AI capex from Microsoft / Meta / Google / Amazon.
- **AI ROI inflection** — enterprise customers reporting AI projects underperforming.
- **Nvidia data-center revenue growth deceleration** — the keystone metric.
- **Fed pivot to cutting** — would broaden the rally beyond AI mega-caps and pull crypto along.

Probable reversal window: **late Q2 / early Q3 2026.** Likely trigger: AI capex disappointment in the August earnings cycle.

### Plain English for 55+ Investors

Bitcoin is down 33% from its September 2025 peak, and you can stop blaming oil or the Fed or some yen-carry-trade boogeyman. The real reason is much simpler: investors are dumping Bitcoin to chase AI stocks. The Nasdaq is at an all-time high while Bitcoin is in the gutter — and those two normally move together. That's a flashing-red sign that says "capital is leaving crypto and parking in Nvidia, Microsoft, and Meta." The good news is that this kind of rotation reverses. The moment AI earnings disappoint — and that day is coming — the same money comes flooding back to Bitcoin. You don't need oil to fall or the Fed to cut. You just need the market to stop believing that hyperscalers can spend $300 billion a year on chips forever.
