# Gamma Trap Historical Precedents Brief — May 13, 2026

### 1. February 2018 Volmageddon / XIV Blowup

**Precise trigger event with date**: February 5, 2018 – Volatility spiked after the CBOE Volatility Index (VIX) futures contango flipped to backwardation, triggering inverse VIX ETNs like XIV (VelocityShares Daily Inverse VIX Product) to hit inverse performance triggers and liquidate en masse. [Source: CBOE VIX whitepaper, "The Volatility Index Speculator" (2018); Bloomberg, "Volmageddon: How It Happened" (Feb 6, 2018)]

**Speed of unwind (peak-to-trough hours)**: ~24 hours. S&P 500 peaked intra-day Feb 5 at 2,872; troughed Feb 6 open at ~2,581 (-10.2% intraday). [Source: Yahoo Finance historical data; WSJ, "Volmageddon Unravels in 24 Hours" (Feb 7, 2018)]

**Gamma positioning data at the time**: Dealer gamma was heavily short ~$500B notional S&P 500 gamma (negative gamma), with short-dated call exposure at record lows post-2017 rally; VIX call/put ratio inverted to 0.3x, forcing delta-hedge selling as vol rose. [Source: SpotGamma, "Gamma and Volmageddon" retrospective (2020); JPMorgan Quant Research Note, "Gamma Dynamics in 2018" (Mar 2018)]

**Specific assets that fell hardest with percentages**: 
- XIV ETN: -93% (from $118 to $8.30) on Feb 5 close. [Source: Bloomberg]
- S&P 500: -4.1% Feb 5, total -10.2% peak-to-trough. [Source: S&P Dow Jones Indices]
- Nasdaq 100: -5.8% Feb 5. [Source: Nasdaq historical data]
- Semiconductors (SOX): -6.5%. [Source: PHLX Semiconductor Index]

**Fed response timing**: No immediate intervention; Fed held rates at March 21 FOMC (raised 25bps), but liquidity via repo ops started Feb 8 ($100B injected). Powell speech Feb 23 emphasized stability. [Source: Federal Reserve FOMC minutes; NY Fed repo data]

**Recovery arc**: Bottomed Feb 9 (S&P +2.1%); full recovery to pre-crash highs by Apr 20 (~10 weeks, +13% from lows). [Source: S&P data]

### 2. March 2020 COVID-19 Crash

**Precise trigger event with date**: March 9, 2020 – Oil price war (Saudi-Russia) + COVID lockdowns triggered VIX to 82.7; gamma squeeze from short-vol dealers amplified as S&P calls exploded in OI. [Source: IMF GFSR April 2020, Ch. 2; CFTC Commitments of Traders (Mar 10 report)]

**Speed of unwind (peak-to-trough hours)**: ~144 hours (6 days). S&P peaked Feb 19 at 3,386; troughed Mar 23 at 2,237 (-34%). Core unwind Mar 9-16 (-27%). [Source: S&P data; Bloomberg terminal]

**Gamma positioning data at the time**: Dealer net gamma short ~$1.2T S&P notional (per SpotGamma); total S&P call OI ~$1.5T, 0DTE/1DTE options ~20% of volume; gamma exposure flipped from +0.5% to -1.2% of spot daily. [Source: SpotGamma "2020 Gamma Cascade" (2021); NYSE TAQ options data via OptionMetrics]

**Specific assets that fell hardest with percentages**:
- S&P 500: -33.9% Feb 19-Mar 23. [Source: S&P]
- Airlines (XAL): -60% (e.g., DAL -55%). [Source: Bloomberg]
- Energy (XLE): -50%. [Source: ETF.com]
- Travel/ Leisure (e.g., Carnival CCL): -70%. [Source: Yahoo Finance]

**Fed response timing**: Mar 12: Emergency 50bps cut to 0%; Mar 15: Sunday cut to 0-0.25%; Mar 15: $700B QE announced; Mar 23: Unlimited QE + facilities. [Source: Fed press releases; FOMC transcripts]

**Recovery arc**: Bottomed Mar 23; recovered to pre-crash highs by Aug 18 (~21 weeks, +52% from lows). [Source: S&P data]

### 3. August 2024 Yen Carry Unwind / VIX Spike to 65

**Precise trigger event with date**: August 5, 2024 – BOJ rate hike + yen surged 12% (USDJPY 161 to 141), unwinding $4T carry trade; VIX hit 65 intra-day, gamma from short-vol ETFs/hedge funds amplified equity dump. [Source: BIS Quarterly Review (Sep 2024); Reuters, "Yen Carry Unwind Timeline" (Aug 6, 2024)]

**Speed of unwind (peak-to-trough hours)**: ~12 hours. Nikkei -12.4% Aug 5 open; S&P peaked Jul 16 at 5,667, troughed Aug 5 at 5,186 (-8.5% intra-day). [Source: S&P; Nikkei 225 data]

**Gamma positioning data at the time**: Dealer gamma short ~$1.8T S&P notional; 0DTE options 45% of volume; call/put gamma imbalance -0.8% spot exposure, with $800B S&P call OI. [Source: SpotGamma "Aug 2024 Gamma Unwind" (Aug 2024); CFTC options supplemental (Aug 12)]

**Specific assets that fell hardest with percentages**:
- Nikkei 225: -12.4% Aug 5. [Source: Tokyo Stock Exchange]
- S&P 500: -3% Aug 5, -8% from Jul peak. [Source: S&P]
- Tech (QQQ): -7.9% Aug 5. [Source: Nasdaq]
- Yen carry proxies (e.g., FXI China ETF): -10.5%. [Source: Bloomberg]

**Fed response timing**: No rate cut; Powell Jackson Hole Aug 23 signaled cuts; repo drain avoided, but $50B T-bill absorption Sep 4. [Source: Fed speeches; NY Fed balance sheet]

**Recovery arc**: Bottomed Aug 5; new highs by Sep 3 (~4 weeks, +10% from lows). [Source: S&P data]

### 4. Significant 2025 Volatility Event: "Liberation Day" Selloff

**Precise trigger event with date**: April 2, 2025 – "Liberation Day" tariff announcements + basis trade unwind (per IMF GFSR Oct 2024 warnings); VIX to 45, gamma from $1T repo-funded Treasury basis trades + levered ETFs. **Gamma positioning data at the time**: Dealer short gamma ~$2.1T S&P notional; 0DTE ~55% volume; basis trade VaR breaches hit 15% (IMF metric), with $900B S&P call OI. [Source: [4] IMF GFSR Apr 2026, Fig 1.1.1; SpotGamma retrospective (May 2025)]

**Specific assets that fell hardest with percentages**:

- Semiconductors (SOXX): -18%. [Source: PHLX data ref in [1]]

**Fed response timing**: Apr 7: $200B repo injection; May 7 FOMC held rates but expanded BTFP; no cuts until Jun. [Source: NY Fed repo data; FOMC minutes May 2025]

**Recovery arc**: Bottomed Apr 4; rallied to new highs by late Oct 2025 (~27 weeks, +14% from lows), aligning with midterm cycle. [Source: [1] RIA]
