### **Key Insights**

#### **1. Volume & Market Dominance**
Hyperliquid has separated itself from the pack, processing over **$175B** in volume over the last 30 days. This accounts for more than **half of the total Perp DEX market share** (approx. 53.8% of the ~$325B total sector volume). In contrast, dYdX and GMX, once the primary leaders, now represent roughly 1% of market share each.

#### **2. Fee Efficiency**
Hyperliquid generated **$54.86M** in fees, dwarfing the combined earnings of its competitors. GMX remains more "fee-dense" relative to its volume ($2.06M fees on $2.89B volume) compared to dYdX ($0.30M fees on $3.83B volume), reflecting GMX's higher take-rate model versus dYdX's high-throughput, lower-fee approach.

#### **3. Open Interest (OI)**
Hyperliquid's Open Interest stands at **$1.84B**, indicating deep liquidity and a high level of sustained trader positioning. This is nearly **8x higher** than dYdX and GMX, suggesting that Hyperliquid is not just capturing high-frequency wash trading but is the preferred venue for institutional-sized directional bets.

#### **4. Ecosystem Trends**
*   **Hyperliquid:** Continues to grow on its dedicated L1, benefiting from vertical integration.
*   **dYdX:** Transitioning almost entirely to its V4 chain; V3 activity has largely dissipated.
*   **GMX:** Volume is now almost entirely concentrated in V2 (Arbitrum and Avalanche), with V1 effectively sunsetting.
*   **Vertex:** Maintains a steady but smaller niche on Arbitrum, trailing the leaders in absolute capital.
