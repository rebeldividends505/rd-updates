$2.6 Trillion. The Gamma Trap Is Loaded.
On May 7, the S&P traded $2.6 trillion in call option notional in a single session — an all-time record. Levered ETFs hold $177 billion in AUM, also all-time high. Critically, that's not just $177B — levered ETF rebalancing pressure scales with leverage squared, so a 3x product (TQQQ, SOXL) generates roughly 9x the daily forced-flow per dollar of AUM compared to a 1x ETF. The destabilizing footprint is closer to a trillion-dollar equivalent. Nomura's Charlie McElligott walked through the mechanic:
McElligott / Nomura, May 7, 2026 "The end of the rally could very likely be a Nasdaq level 1 'limit-down'-type trade, and/or with SMH -15% to -20% in a single-session, and slowly moving into Pretty Damn Real Delta."
The Calendar Coincidence Nobody's Talking About
What makes Friday May 15 specific isn't just Powell. It's the calendar. May 15 is also monthly options expiration — the day most of that $2.6 trillion in S&P calls comes off the books. The dealer long-gamma position that's been suppressing volatility for weeks rolls off in the same session the Fed leadership changes hands. Whether or not McElligott is right about the trigger, the structural support that's been holding the rally together is mechanically scheduled to disappear on the same day a new Fed Chair is sworn in.
This is McElligott's analysis from Nomura's desk, not a prediction by Rebel Dividends. We are showing it because it explains why the rotation is mechanically loaded right now.
