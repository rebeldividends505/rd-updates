As of May 2026, the Hyperliquid HIP-2 (Hyperliquidity) spot market functions as a core component of the protocol's "HYPE flywheel," integrating automated market-making directly into the L1's central limit order book.

### 1. Revenue Model: HIP-2 Spot Markets
The HIP-2 model is designed to provide "instant liquidity" for native tokens (HIP-1 assets) without requiring manual market makers.
*   **Mechanism:** It is a protocol-level strategy that places resting bid and ask orders on the order book. It maintains a **0.3% spread** and updates every 3 seconds based on oracle prices.
*   **Fee Structure:** Spot trades typically carry a **0.045% Taker fee** and a **0.015% Maker fee**. To incentivize spot adoption, trading volume on spot markets counts **2x** toward a user's volume-weighted fee discounts.
*   **Asset Burning:** Fees collected in native HIP-1 tokens (like HYPE) are frequently **burned directly**, creating immediate deflationary pressure for those specific assets.

### 3. Fee Distribution: Where the Money Goes
Hyperliquid distributes **100% of protocol fees** to the ecosystem. The current distribution for standard markets is:

*   **Assistance Fund (HYPE Buyback): 99%**
    The vast majority of fees go to the Assistance Fund. This fund programmatically uses USDC revenue to buy back **HYPE** tokens from the spot market and **permanently burns** them.
*   **HLP (Hyperliquidity Provider) Vault: 1%**
    A small portion of trading fees is directed to HLP depositors to supplement their returns. (Note: This was reduced from 3% to 1% in late 2025).
*   **Validators: 0% (Direct Trading Fees)**
    Validators do not receive a share of the trading fees. Instead, they are compensated through **new token issuance (staking rewards)** and **network gas fees**. This separation ensures that trading revenue is fully utilized to support the HYPE token's value.
