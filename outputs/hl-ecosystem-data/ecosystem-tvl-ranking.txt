### **Infrastructure Guide: HyperCore vs. HyperEVM**

The Hyperliquid L1 uses a unique dual-architecture. While all data is secured by the same consensus, they serve different functional roles:

*   **HyperCore (The Financial Hub):**
    *   **Role:** High-speed execution engine for the order book.
    *   **Protocols:** **HLP**, **Hyperliquid Spot**, and the **Native Bridge**.
    *   **Tech:** Custom Rust-based VM; handles 200k+ orders per second with sub-second finality.
*   **HyperEVM (The Application Layer):**
    *   **Role:** Fully Ethereum-compatible smart contract environment.
    *   **Protocols:** **Kinetiq**, **HyperLend**, **Felix**, and **Morpho**.
    *   **Tech:** Solidity-based; allows DeFi protocols to compose directly with HyperCore liquidity via native "precompiles" (CoreReader).

### **Key Ecosystem Insights**
*   **LST Dominance:** Liquid staking (kHYPE and stHYPE) represents a massive portion of HyperEVM TVL, as users seek to earn staking rewards while keeping their HYPE liquid for lending or trading.
*   **Native Liquidity:** The **HLP (Hyperliquid Liquidity Provider)** remains the most critical primitive, as it acts as the counterparty for traders on the perp exchange and yields a significant portion of protocol fees.
*   **Expansion into Lending:** Protocols like **HyperLend** and **Morpho** have successfully captured the demand for leverage within the ecosystem, allowing users to borrow USDC against their HYPE holdings to "loop" their exposure.
