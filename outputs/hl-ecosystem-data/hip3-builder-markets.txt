Hyperliquid's **HIP-3 (Hyperliquid Improvement Proposal 3)**, also known as **"Builder-Deployed Perpetuals,"** is a protocol upgrade that allows independent developers to permissionlessly launch and manage their own perpetual futures markets. This shift transformed Hyperliquid from a single-venue exchange into a decentralized infrastructure layer for "Exchange-as-a-Service" (EaaS).

### 1. What is HIP-3?
HIP-3 enables anyone to become a **Market Deployer**. Unlike native Hyperliquid markets (which are validator-voted and use the protocol-wide HLP vault for liquidity), HIP-3 markets are isolated and managed by the builder. This allows for the listing of a wide variety of assets, including niche crypto tokens, pre-IPO stocks, commodities, and foreign exchange (FX) pairs.

### 2. Market and Builder Statistics
As of May 2026, the Hyperliquid ecosystem has expanded significantly through HIP-3.

*   **Number of HIP-3 Markets:** There are currently dozens of active HIP-3 markets. While the exact number fluctuates as builders launch or decommission markets, the protocol supports hundreds of potential listing slots.
*   **Top Builders/Integrators:**
    *   **Kinetiq:** A leading EaaS provider that allows $HYPE holders to "crowdfund" the required 500,000 $HYPE staking bond for new markets in exchange for a share of the fees.
    *   **Unit:** A major builder specializing in high-performance spot and perp markets on the Hyperliquid L1.
    *   **Kinto:** An L2 that integrates with Hyperliquid for institutional-grade access.

### 4. Revenue Sharing Mechanics
The economic model of HIP-3 is designed to align builders with the protocol:

*   **The 50/50 Split:** All trading fees generated on a HIP-3 market are split evenly:
    *   **50% to the Builder (Market Deployer):** This incentivizes builders to attract liquidity and volume.
    *   **50% to the Hyperliquid Protocol:** These funds typically go to the **Assistance Fund**, which uses the majority (~99%) of its revenue to buy back and burn/distribute the native **$HYPE** token.
*   **Double Base Fees:** To ensure the protocol earns as much as it would on a native market, HIP-3 markets default to roughly double the standard fee rate (e.g., 9 bps taker).
*   **Growth Mode:** Builders can choose to slash taker fees (often to <0.01%) to bootstrap their market. This reduces immediate revenue but is used to drive massive volume and capture market share.
*   **Builder Codes:** Separate from HIP-3, any developer (even without a 500k $HYPE stake) can add a "Builder Fee" of up to **0.1%** on perp trades via their frontend or trading bot, which is paid directly to them by the user.

### 5. Builder Requirements
Launching a HIP-3 market is permissionless but requires a significant economic commitment:
*   **Staking Bond:** A deployer must stake **500,000 $HYPE** tokens.
*   **Slashing Risk:** This bond acts as collateral. If a builder mismanages their oracle or acts maliciously, validators can slash this stake.
*   **Listing Auctions:** While the first few markets are free for a deployer, additional slots are secured via Dutch auctions paid in $HYPE.
