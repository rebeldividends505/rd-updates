Hyperliquid's DeFi ecosystem has expanded significantly, with **HyperLend** currently serving as the flagship lending protocol on the network. As of May 18, 2026, the lending landscape on Hyperliquid is characterized by a mix of native money markets and multi-chain protocols that have expanded to the Hyperliquid L1.

### **What is HyperLend?**
HyperLend is the primary decentralized money market native to the Hyperliquid L1 (HyperEVM). It operates similarly to Aave, utilizing a pooled-to-peer model where users can supply assets to earn yield or use them as collateral to borrow. 

**Key Features:**
*   **Modular Architecture:** Consists of **Core (Pooled)** markets for major assets and **Isolated** markets for more niche or volatile assets.
*   **Asset Alignment:** Recently transitioned to **native USDC** as its primary stablecoin quote asset following the integration of Circle's treasury on Hyperliquid.
*   **Token ($HPL):** The native governance token, $HPL, allows for borrower rebates and interest rate discounts when staked.
*   **TVL:** Currently holds approximately **$416.9M** in total value locked across its pooled and isolated versions.

### **Analysis & Recent Developments**
1.  **USDC Dominance:** The ecosystem has moved away from older stablecoins (like USDH) in favor of native USDC. Yield from the official USDC treasury on Hyperliquid is notably used for **HYPE buybacks**, creating a unique circular economy for lenders.
2.  **Collateral Innovation:** HyperLend has begun supporting **ERC-4626 Tokenized Vaults**, allowing users to use their Hyperliquid DEX liquidity provider (HLP) positions as collateral.
3.  **Efficiency Mode (E-Mode):** High-efficiency borrowing is available for correlated assets (e.g., HYPE vs. staked HYPE), allowing for LTVs as high as **81.5%**.

*Note: TVL and rates are as of May 18, 2026. Rates fluctuate based on real-time market demand and protocol utilization.*
