Hyperliquid’s **HIP-3 (Hyperliquid Improvement Proposal 3)**, also known as "Builder-Deployed Perpetuals," allows developers to permissionlessly deploy their own perpetual futures markets. While **trade.xyz** is currently the most dominant builder (specializing in tokenized stocks and synthetic indices), several other builders are active or in the pipeline.

### Supporting Infrastructure Builders
These projects do not necessarily deploy perp markets themselves but provide the essential infrastructure for HIP-3 builders to launch and scale:

*   **Kinetiq:** A liquid staking and "Exchange-as-a-Service" protocol. It allows retail users to pool **HYPE** to help builders meet the **500,000 HYPE** staking bond required to launch a new HIP-3 DEX. (TVL: ~$897M across kHYPE and Earn).
*   **Hydromancer (HyperDash):** A data and API stack that provides full monitoring and orderbook data for HIP-3 DEXes.
*   **Unit:** A liquidity management protocol supporting the deployment and staking requirements for new builders.

### Key Requirements for HIP-3 Builders
To deploy a new market on Hyperliquid, builders must adhere to the following framework:
1.  **Staking Bond:** A mandatory **500,000 HYPE** tokens must be staked as a security/slashing bond.
2.  **Asset Listing:** The first 3 assets are free; subsequent listings are determined via a **Dutch Auction**.
3.  **Revenue Share:** Builders retain **50% of the trading fees** generated on their specific markets.
4.  **Ownership:** Builders are responsible for their own front-end interfaces and oracle feed management.
