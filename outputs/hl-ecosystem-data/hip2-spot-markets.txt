### **HIP-2: How Mechanical Bootstrapping Works**

HIP-2, or **Hyperliquidity**, solves the "liquidity cold start" problem by embedding a market-making strategy into the **HyperCore** execution engine.

#### **1. The Mechanics**
Unlike Uniswap (which uses a passive $x \times y = k$ formula), HIP-2 operates on a **Central Limit Order Book (CLOB)**.
*   **Automated Grid Trading:** The protocol automatically places a "grid" of limit orders around the current price.
*   **3-Second Refresh:** Orders are canceled and replaced every block (approx. 3 seconds) to track price movements.
*   **Recursive Pricing:** Price levels are defined geometrically ($px_i = px_{i-1} \times 1.003$), ensuring a consistent **0.3% spread**.

#### **2. Liquidity Bootstrapping Process**
When a new token launches (often via a **HIP-1 Dutch Auction**), HIP-2 provides instant liquidity:
*   **Seeding the Buy Side:** A portion of the USDC raised in the token's initial auction is automatically deposited into HIP-2 to fund "bid" orders.
*   **Seeding the Sell Side:** A portion of the token's total supply is allocated to the engine to fund "ask" orders.
*   **Protocol-Owned Liquidity:** This liquidity is typically **permanently locked**, preventing "rug pulls" and ensuring there is always a "floor" for users to trade against.

#### **3. Why It’s Unique**
*   **Validator-Secured:** The market-making logic is part of the blockchain's L1 consensus, not a third-party bot.
*   **Efficiency:** It provides the passive experience of an AMM for LPs but the execution efficiency of a professional market maker for traders.
*   **Revenue Sharing:** 97% of the fees generated from these trades are used to **buy back and burn HYPE**, directly linking spot market activity to token value.
