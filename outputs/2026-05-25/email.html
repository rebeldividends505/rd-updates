Tax Treatment: Return of Capital treatment under IRC §301(c)(2) is available only in years where corporate distributions exceed current and accumulated earnings and profits; classification can change year-to-year based on corporate performance. Return-of-Capital distributions reduce a shareholder's adjusted tax basis in their stock; any distribution in excess of adjusted basis is taxed as capital gain. Rebel Dividends Corporation has classified 2026 distributions as Return of Capital; shareholders will receive a corrected 1099-DIV reflecting Box 3 treatment. Return of Capital is not the same as tax-free — it defers taxation by reducing cost basis. Consult your tax advisor regarding how this classification applies to your individual circumstances.

Fee Structure: Rebel Dividends Corporation charges a 20% management fee on all dividends distributed to shareholders. The 20% withholding referenced in projection math reflects this management/operating reserve, not a federal income tax. Dividends are characterized as Return of Capital and are generally not currently taxable as ordinary income, though they reduce your cost basis.
